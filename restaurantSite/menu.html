Gallo Pinto
Much more than rice and beans, gallo pinto is a national dish that Nicaraguans can happily eat for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. The most popular dish amongst all nicaraguans.
$5

Desayuno Nica
Gallo Pinto is one of the main components of a desayuno típico, a traditional Nicaraguan breakfast, which is loaded with enough energy to fill you up until well past lunch.
$9

Nacatamales
Made from maize dough stuffed with pork, slices of potato, and a sprig of a minty herb called yerba buena, nacatamales are Nicaragua’s version of the Mexican tamal or the Venezuelan hallaca.
$8

Quesillo
Cheesy treats sold from street carts all over the country. They consist of a tortilla wrapped around a circle of white cheese smothered with curtido.
$4

Indio Viejo
A thick soup made of maize dough with shredded beef and onion, Indio Viejo (Old Indian) is a Nicaraguan dish that has its roots in mayan culture.
$7

Vigorón
A mini-mountain of boiled yuca and chicharrones (pork rinds) topped with a chopped cabbage salad that contains slices of mimbro, a sharp-tasting fruit.
$7
